Poverty vs. Lack of Healthcare




Correlation Discovered Between Poverty and Health Risks

This plot identifies key correlations between Poverty and Health Risks by associating Poverty % vs. Lack of Healthcare % by State. By hovering over each state, you can see the direct correlation between lower poverty % and lower % of persons that lack healthcare. Hence the argument could be made that those states with lower poverty levels also have lower % of people who lack healthcare.

In observing the data set we can see the direct correlation of this hypothesis by examining 2 states in particular. Massachusetts has a Poverty Level of 11.6% and those lacking in Healthcare are at a low 4.5%. Conversely, in the state of Texas, the Poverty Level is 17.2% and those that lack Healthcare are ranked at 24.9%.

Other factors such as access to healthcare services, age, and population sample may also impact these correlations.
